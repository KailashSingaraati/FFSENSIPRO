⚙️ Complete Settings Guide

🎯 What is Sensitivity in Free Fire? ▼

Sensitivity in Free Fire controls how fast your camera and aim moves when you swipe on your screen. Higher sensitivity = faster movement but harder to aim precisely. Lower sensitivity = slower but more accurate.

  • General: Camera movement when not aiming down sights
  • Red Dot: Sensitivity when using red dot sight
  • 2x Scope: Sensitivity with 2x zoom scope
  • 4x Scope: Sensitivity with 4x zoom scope
  • AWM Scope: Sensitivity for AWM sniper scope
  • Free Look: Camera speed when using free look button

🕹️ Best HUD Layout Setup ▼

A good HUD layout is crucial for fast reaction and headshots. Here's the recommended layout:

  • Fire Button: Large, bottom-right corner
  • Jump: Left side, thumb reach
  • Crouch: Near fire button
  • Scope/ADS: Left side of fire
  • Reload: Center-bottom
  • Free Look: Left side
  • Weapons: Right bottom row

Tip: Use 4-finger or 5-finger claw setup for best performance. Practice for 2 weeks to get used to it.

🔊 Sound Settings for Competitive Play ▼

Sound is a HUGE advantage in Free Fire. Use headphones for best results.

  • Footstep Volume: Max (100) — hear enemies approaching
  • Music: 0 — reduces distraction
  • Effects Volume: 80-100
  • Use 3D Audio: On if available
  • Voice Chat: Team only
🎯 How to Improve Headshot Rate ▼
  • Keep crosshair at head/chest level always
  • Use gyroscope for micro-corrections
  • Practice in Training Mode for 15 mins daily
  • Use M1014 or M500 to build headshot habit
  • Don't panic spray — tap fire for headshots
  • Move while shooting to make yourself harder to hit
  • Learn enemy head hitbox position for each character
📱 Developer Options for Gaming (Android) ▼

Enable Developer Options: Settings → About Phone → Tap Build Number 7 times

Then set these:

  • Window Animation Scale: 0.5x or Off
  • Transition Animation Scale: 0.5x or Off
  • Animator Duration Scale: 0.5x or Off
  • Force GPU Rendering: On
  • Background Process Limit: 2 or Standard

⚠️ Warning: Only change these settings if you know what you're doing.
